Summary: Latitude Vs. Humidity (%)

Snopsis:

Humidity is defined as the amount of water vapor in the air. If there is a lot of ware vapor in the air, the humidity will be high. Reliaive Humidity is the amount of water vapor actually suspended in the air expressed as a percentage of the maximum amount of water the air can hold at the same temperature.

We would expect that the closer to 0 latitude you are, the higher the content of water vapor suspended in the air. At extreme latitudes, the molecules move slow enough that the water molecules would fall out of suspension either as rain or snow depending on the temperature. With higher constant temperatures, the air can support the weight of more water. This scatter chart shows a higher consentration of cities with above 50% humidity clusterd within ±40° of 0 latitude.
