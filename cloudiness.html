Summary: Latitude Vs. Cloudiness (%)

Cloud cover as it relates to latitude, often have varying degrees of complexity. On one hand, high cloud cover can mean cooler temperatures because the sun cannot heat the atmosphere. On the other hand, cloud cover can trap heat when the sun isn't shinning to keep temperatures more moderate. What we've found according to this scanner plot proves only that clouds are omnipresent.

Should there have been a study of Temperature Vs. Cloudiness, then I believe that we would have seen a completely different result. Lower tempreatures, on average result in clearer skys while the normal convection caused from higher temperatures mean a greater amount of humidity in the air which can lead to higher cloud coverage. Therefor there is no descernable effect on cloudiness based on latitude in this analysis.
